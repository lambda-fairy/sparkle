Sparkle

A project planning tool for secondary students

Sparkle is an online planning tool designed for high school technology students. It aims to help students manage the time they spend on their projects, and guide them through the thought processes needed for them to succeed.

It is a work in progress, and is not usable at this stage.

Why do we need yet another planning tool?

The worlds of work and school differ in many ways. For example:

  • A professional knows what he's doing, and appreciates the value of following a process and planning ahead. A student doesn't.
  • A professional may work on many projects at once. A student only works on one (and often won't complete it).
  • Other minor things: student work needs to be handed in and marked, which necessitates an export feature. Many students keep a daily log—we'll need to support that.

Anything interesting from a technical point of view?

The backend is written in Haskell, a totally not mainstream but very well designed programming language.

All data is held in memory, rather than in a separate database. This simplifies setting up and makes the server very fast.
